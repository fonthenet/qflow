## 5. Processor Obligations

5.1 **Confidentiality.** The Processor shall ensure that persons authorised to Process the Processor Personal Data are subject to appropriate obligations of confidentiality.

5.2 **Security.** The Processor shall implement and maintain the technical and organisational measures set out in Annex B (Security Measures).

5.3 **Sub-processors.** The Processor shall not engage Sub-processors without prior general written authorisation from the Controller. The current list of authorised Sub-processors is set out in Annex A. The Processor shall notify the Controller of any intended changes (additions or replacements) at least 30 days in advance. If the Controller objects to a new Sub-processor on reasonable data protection grounds, the parties shall negotiate in good faith.

5.4 **Data Subject Rights.** The Processor shall, taking into account the nature of the Processing, assist the Controller (by appropriate technical and organisational measures insofar as possible) in fulfilling the Controller's obligation to respond to requests for exercising Data Subjects' rights under the GDPR (including access, rectification, erasure, restriction, portability, and objection).

5.5 **Data Protection Impact Assessments.** The Processor shall provide reasonable cooperation to the Controller in relation to any data protection impact assessments or prior consultations required under Articles 35-36 GDPR.

5.6 **Notification of Breaches.** The Processor shall notify the Controller without undue delay (and in any event within 72 hours of becoming aware) after becoming aware of a Personal Data Breach affecting Processor Personal Data. Notification shall include: (i) description of the nature of the breach; (ii) categories and approximate number of Data Subjects and records concerned; (iii) name and contact details of the DPO or other contact; (iv) likely consequences; (v) measures taken or proposed.

5.7 **Deletion or Return.** Upon termination of the Services, the Processor shall, at the Controller's election, delete or return all Processor Personal Data (including copies), unless Union or Member State law requires retention. The Processor shall certify such deletion in writing upon request.

5.8 **Audit.** The Processor shall make available to the Controller all information necessary to demonstrate compliance with this DPA, and shall allow for and contribute to audits and inspections by the Controller or its mandated auditors, subject to reasonable advance notice and confidentiality obligations. The Processor may satisfy audit obligations through provision of up-to-date SOC 2 Type II reports or equivalent certifications.

## Annex B — Security Measures

The Processor implements and maintains the following technical and organisational security measures:

### B.1 Encryption
- All data in transit protected by TLS 1.2 or higher.
- All data at rest encrypted using AES-256 via Supabase (AWS) managed encryption.
- Authentication tokens encrypted and signed using industry-standard algorithms (RS256 JWT).

### B.2 Access Controls
- Role-based access control (RBAC) enforced at application layer and database level.
- Row-Level Security (RLS) policies in Supabase prevent cross-tenant data access.
- Principle of least privilege applied to all staff and system accounts.
- Multi-factor authentication available and recommended for all admin accounts.

### B.3 Availability and Resilience
- Database hosted on managed cloud infrastructure with automated backups.
- Redundant infrastructure with failover capabilities provided by Supabase/AWS.
- Regular automated backups with point-in-time recovery capability.

### B.4 Personnel and Process
- Access to production systems limited to authorised personnel only.
- Staff with access to Personal Data are subject to confidentiality obligations.
- Regular security dependency audits of application code.
- Incident response procedure with designated breach notification owner.

### B.5 Breach Notification
- Processor maintains an incident response procedure.
- Controller notified within 72 hours of Processor becoming aware of a breach affecting Processor Personal Data.
- Breach notification includes the information required under Article 33(3) GDPR.

### B.6 Testing
- Security-relevant changes are reviewed before deployment.
- Dependency vulnerability scanning performed on the CI/CD pipeline.
