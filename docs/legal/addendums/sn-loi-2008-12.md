# Senegal — Loi n° 2008-12 du 25 janvier 2008 sur la Protection des Données à Caractère Personnel

> LAWYER REVIEW REQUIRED before relying on this summary for customer commitments.

## Overview

Senegal enacted **Loi n° 2008-12 du 25 janvier 2008 sur la Protection des Données à Caractère Personnel**, one of the earliest comprehensive data protection laws in Sub-Saharan Africa. The supervisory authority is the **Commission de Protection des Données Personnelles (CDP)**, established under Article 4 of the law. Senegal is a signatory to the ECOWAS Supplementary Act on Personal Data Protection (2010), which provides a regional framework. The Senegalese law is closely modelled on the French Loi Informatique et Libertés / EU Directive 95/46/EC framework.

## Key Obligations

**Consent model:** Processing requires the data subject's free, specific, and informed consent (Article 17). Exceptions apply for legal obligation, contract performance, vital interests, public interest, and legitimate interest — analogous to the EU Directive framework.

**Data subject rights:** Access (Article 25), rectification (Article 26), erasure and objection (Article 28). Controllers must respond within 8 days for access requests relating to data likely to impact individual rights, and within 2 months for other access requests.

**Data localization / cross-border transfers:** Article 54 restricts transfer of personal data to countries that do not ensure an adequate level of protection. The CDP determines adequacy. EU member states are generally considered adequate. Transfers to non-adequate countries require CDP authorization or specific derogations (contractual clauses, data subject consent). Supabase US infrastructure requires contractual safeguards; EU region likely adequate.

**Registration:** Controllers must file a declaration with the CDP before commencing processing (Article 36). Processing operations presenting particular risks require prior CDP authorization (Article 39). Senegalese businesses using Qflo should register as controllers.

**Sensitive data:** Health, racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, and criminal records are sensitive categories requiring prior CDP authorization.

**Breach notification:** Loi 2008-12 does not specify breach notification requirements explicitly. CDP guidance should be consulted; apply conservative 72-hour standard.

**ECOWAS framework:** Senegal's law must be read alongside the ECOWAS Supplementary Act A/SA.1/01/10, which provides a harmonized regional framework for the Economic Community of West African States.

## Qflo-Specific Actions

- Inform Senegalese tenants of CDP declaration obligation.

- Privacy notices in French (official language); Wolof and other local languages optional but good practice.
- Health data for clinic vertical: prior CDP authorization required.
