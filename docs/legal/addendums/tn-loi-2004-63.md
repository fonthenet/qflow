# Tunisia — Loi organique n° 2004-63 du 27 juillet 2004 portant sur la protection des données à caractère personnel

> LAWYER REVIEW REQUIRED before relying on this summary for customer commitments.

## Overview

Tunisia was a pioneer in African data protection, enacting **Loi organique 2004-63** before most African peers. The supervisory authority is the **Instance Nationale de Protection des Données à Caractère Personnel (INPDP)**. Tunisia is also a signatory to Council of Europe Convention 108.

## Key Obligations

**Consent model:** Processing of personal data requires explicit, informed, and prior consent from the data subject (Article 6) unless processing is based on: legal obligation, contractual necessity, vital interests, or public interest. Automated processing of sensitive data (health, religion, political opinion, biometrics) requires INPDP authorization.

**Data subject rights:** Data subjects have the right to information (Articles 7-9), access (Article 13), rectification and deletion (Article 15), and objection (Article 16). Access requests must be responded to within one month (extendable to three months for complex cases).

**Data localization / cross-border transfers:** Article 50 requires authorization from the INPDP before transferring personal data abroad to countries not deemed to have adequate protection. Controllers must demonstrate that the recipient country's legal framework provides adequate protection equivalent to Loi 2004-63. Transfers to the EU may benefit from reasoned adequacy assessment given the EU's GDPR framework, but formal INPDP authorization is still good practice.

**Registration / authorization:** Controllers must file a declaration with the INPDP before establishing any automated personal data processing system (Article 19). Processing of sensitive data categories requires prior INPDP authorization (Article 20).

**Breach notification:** Loi 2004-63 does not specify breach notification timelines. Apply conservative 72-hour standard; notify INPDP promptly upon discovering significant breaches.

**Penalties:** Violations may result in criminal penalties under Articles 57-65, including fines and imprisonment for offences such as unlawful processing, failure to comply with INPDP orders, and unauthorized data transfers abroad.

## Qflo-Specific Actions

- Inform Tunisian tenants of their obligation to declare processing activities to INPDP.
- Sensitive categories (e.g. health data for clinic verticals) require prior INPDP authorization.
- Cross-border data transfers require INPDP authorization or demonstrated adequacy.
- Privacy notices in French and Arabic; Arabic is the official language of Tunisian law.
- Include INPDP complaint right in all privacy notices for Tunisian data subjects.
