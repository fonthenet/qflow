# Kingdom of Saudi Arabia — Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL)

> LAWYER REVIEW REQUIRED before relying on this summary for customer commitments.

## Overview

Saudi Arabia enacted the **Personal Data Protection Law (PDPL)** by Royal Decree No. M/19 dated 9/2/1443H (corresponding to 16 September 2021). Implementing regulations were issued and the law became enforceable on 14 September 2023 after a two-year transitional period. The supervisory authority is the **Saudi Data and Artificial Intelligence Authority (SDAIA)** / **National Data Management Office (NDMO)**. The PDPL has extraterritorial reach — it applies to any processing of personal data relating to individuals in Saudi Arabia, regardless of where the controller is established.

## Key Obligations

**Consent model:** Consent must be explicit, and for sensitive personal data, express written consent is required (Articles 4-5 PDPL). Legitimate purposes for processing without consent are narrowly defined and include: contractual necessity, vital interests, legal claims, and compliance with legal obligations. Consent must be documented and must be as easy to withdraw as to give.

**Data subject rights:** Rights of access (Article 12), correction and completion (Article 13), destruction of data no longer needed (Article 14), and objection to processing (Article 15). Controllers must respond within 30 days; this can be extended once with notice.

**Data localization / cross-border transfers:** Article 29 of the implementing regulations restricts transfers of personal data outside Saudi Arabia. Transfers are permitted only where: (a) SDAIA approves the transfer; (b) the destination country has adequate protection as recognized by SDAIA; (c) the transfer is necessary for a contract or legal claim; or (d) the data subject consents. **SDAIA has not published a full adequacy list as of the knowledge cutoff.** Cross-border transfer risk is material for Qflo's cloud-based architecture. Legal review required before onboarding Saudi enterprise customers.

**Data residency:** For government and critical sector data, additional localization requirements may apply under sector-specific regulations (e.g. regulations from SAMA for financial data, NDMO for government data). Qflo's public sector and banking verticals in KSA carry elevated residency risk.

**Breach notification:** Within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach that may cause harm to data subjects, controllers must notify SDAIA (Article 28 PDPL Implementing Regulations). Notification to data subjects where risk is high.

**Penalties:** Administrative fines up to SAR 5 million (approximately USD 1.33M); fines may be doubled for repeat violations.

## Qflo-Specific Actions

- Material cross-border transfer risk: advise KSA customers accordingly; obtain legal opinion on SDAIA-approved mechanisms.
- Government and banking verticals: additional sector regulation review required before onboarding.
- Arabic-language privacy notices mandatory for KSA data subjects.
- Consent must be documented; WhatsApp opt-in must be recorded with timestamp.
- Monitor SDAIA guidance as the regime matures; adequacy decisions may simplify transfers.
