# UAE — Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the Protection of Personal Data (PDPL)

> LAWYER REVIEW REQUIRED before relying on this summary for customer commitments.

## Overview

The UAE enacted its first federal data protection law, **Federal Decree-Law No. 45 of 2021 on the Protection of Personal Data (PDPL)**, which came into force on 2 January 2022. The Executive Regulation was issued by Cabinet Decision No. 33 of 2022. The supervisory authority is the **UAE Data Office** (formerly the UAE Data Protection Office). Note: The UAE has free zones (DIFC, ADGM) with separate, more mature data protection regimes — the DIFC Data Protection Law 2020 and ADGM Data Protection Regulations 2021. Entities operating in those free zones should apply the respective free zone law.

## Key Obligations

**Consent model:** Consent must be explicit, clear, and specific (Article 6 PDPL). Processing may also be based on: contractual necessity, legal obligation, vital interests, legitimate interest, or public interest. Consent withdrawal must be as easy as giving consent.

**Data subject rights:** Rights of access (Article 13), correction (Article 14), deletion (Article 15), restriction (Article 16), data portability (Article 17), and objection (Article 18). Controllers must respond within 30 days (extendable to 60 days for complex requests). Complaints may be lodged with the UAE Data Office.

**Data localization / cross-border transfers:** Article 26 PDPL permits transfers outside the UAE only to countries with adequate protection (as approved by the UAE Data Office), or with the data subject's consent, or under contractual safeguards approved by the UAE Data Office. The UAE Data Office has not published a comprehensive adequacy list as of the knowledge cutoff. **Practical risk:** Supabase EU and US infrastructure for UAE customers may require UAE Data Office approved safeguards. Local counsel review strongly recommended before onboarding UAE enterprise customers.

**Sensitive data:** Health data, biometric data, financial data, and data related to children require additional safeguards and explicit consent.

**Breach notification:** Within 72 hours of becoming aware of a breach affecting data subjects' rights (Article 23). Notify the UAE Data Office and, where risk is high, affected data subjects.

**Penalties:** Administrative fines up to AED 20 million; criminal referrals possible for certain offences.

**Children:** Processing data of persons under 18 requires verifiable parental consent.

## DIFC / ADGM Note

Entities in the Dubai International Financial Centre (DIFC) apply the DIFC Data Protection Law 2020 and its Implementing Regulations — effectively GDPR-aligned, administered by the DIFC Commissioner of Data Protection. ADGM entities apply the ADGM Data Protection Regulations 2021, also GDPR-aligned. These are more mature and enforcement-active regimes than the mainland PDPL.

## Qflo-Specific Actions

- Cross-border transfer: advise UAE customers (mainland) of transfer restrictions; explore UAE Data Office approved mechanisms.
- DIFC/ADGM customers: apply DPA with GDPR-equivalent terms.
- Privacy notices available in English and Arabic.
- Children's data: build age gate or parental consent flow if any vertical serves minors.
- Health data in clinic vertical: explicit consent, segregated processing.
