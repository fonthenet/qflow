# Côte d'Ivoire — Loi n° 2013-450 du 19 juin 2013 relative à la Protection des Données à Caractère Personnel

> LAWYER REVIEW REQUIRED before relying on this summary for customer commitments.

## Overview

Côte d'Ivoire enacted **Loi n° 2013-450 du 19 juin 2013 relative à la Protection des Données à Caractère Personnel**. The supervisory authority is the **Autorité de Régulation des Télécommunications/TIC de Côte d'Ivoire (ARTCI)**, which also handles data protection alongside telecommunications regulation. Côte d'Ivoire participates in the ECOWAS regional data protection framework under the Supplementary Act A/SA.1/01/10 of 2010.

## Key Obligations

**Consent model:** Processing requires free, specific, and informed consent of the data subject (Article 10). Exceptions to consent include: legal obligation, contract performance, vital interests, public interest mission, and legitimate interest — consistent with the EU Directive 95/46/EC model that influenced the law.

**Data subject rights:** Rights of information (Articles 12-13), access (Article 18), rectification (Article 19), and opposition (Article 20). Response to access requests within 30 days.

**Data localization / cross-border transfers:** Article 38 restricts transfers to countries without adequate protection. ARTCI determines adequacy. Transfers to non-adequate countries require: (a) ARTCI authorization; (b) contractual safeguards; or (c) specific derogations (consent, vital interests, contract). Transfers to EU are generally considered adequate. Supabase US infrastructure requires contractual safeguards.

**Registration / authorization:** Controllers must declare their processing activities to ARTCI (Article 25) before starting processing. Processing of sensitive data or certain high-risk categories requires prior ARTCI authorization (Article 27). Ivorian businesses using Qflo should register as controllers with ARTCI.

**Sensitive data:** Health data, racial/ethnic origin, political opinions, religious beliefs, trade union membership, and criminal records are special categories requiring explicit consent and ARTCI authorization.

**Security:** Controllers must implement appropriate technical and organizational security measures (Article 22) and must notify ARTCI of security incidents that compromise the confidentiality, integrity, or availability of personal data.

**Penalties:** Non-compliance may result in administrative sanctions by ARTCI and criminal penalties under the Ivorian Penal Code as applicable.
